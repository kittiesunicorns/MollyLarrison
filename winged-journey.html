A Winged Journey

I. a new beginning

A strange choice, on that dark day

to hitch a ride on the highway

sleeping, with one eye open

pinned beneath a bed of patrons

cracking the door to all thatâ€™s broken

Crossing was neither the River Styx

nor fire with blazing seas

it showered apostolic apologies

landing soft, on one knee

in ample attitude—laden with regret

Drinking the spiked notions of deepening purpose

assuming permission, not objection

assuming acceptance, not rejection

and still not knowing the depth of the fall

due to the randomness of it all

You followed me here, from a great unknown

to a garden with singing rain, where heavens sigh, relieved

beyond the streets and viaducts made of stone

a shattered bias of what it should have been

a slightly ambiguous regret of what it might have been

somewhat exposed to a truth from

somewhere before, long past

in shadows that obscured

our most familiar light.

II. midjourney

You followed me because it seemed innocent

we all followed because we couldn't help ourselves

to look away from the parade of madness was impossible,

like a hot-mess witness of irresistible pageantry

At first, in observation, the presumed promise of our attention

after every person who had cared stopped noticing

that you were gone from the present—

the question arose whether you cared for anyone, ever.

even in our greatest self-reflection,

scurrying down rabbit holes in search of the truth

the disappearance for some time,

granted us bearings and forgiveness and insight
