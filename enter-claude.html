Subscription. So. Many. Subscriptions. This brings me to my first intentional expansion of the year: I’m getting cozy with Claude. While I’ve spent my career inside the Google ecosystem, I’ve realized that a single perspective isn't enough to navigate the complexity of this new era. I need a thought partner—or a crew of two, to be precise.

I’m not leaving Gemini; if anything, I’m strengthening its role. I think Claude and Gemini will make the perfect duo: responsible, acutely funny, and multi-modal. (I’m particularly loving the graphics from Nano Banana!) If Gemini is my data powerhouse and Google Workspace engine, Claude is the philosopher stepping into the booth next door.

Why I’m adding Claude to the mix:

  • A Shared Ethos: Anthropic has proven repeatedly that they care about human-centric AI.
  • The Thoughtful Provocateur: Dario Amodei, Anthropic's CEO, is a voice I value. While I don’t agree with everything he writes, I appreciate his willingness to be publicly wrong and his commitment to bold warnings.
  • Peer Validation: I’ve heard consistently positive feedback from engineers at Google who use Claude alongside Gemini to verify logic and code.
  • A Lab Mindset: I prefer technology that feels like it’s being developed in a lab rather than a race track. I’d rather keep the "stealth bombers" grounded until we understand their trajectory.

Taking it for a spin

The first thing that struck me about the Claude UI was the clarity of its intent. When I subscribed, I was immediately greeted by a Billing section that makes it as easy to cancel a plan as it is to start one. In an era of "dark patterns," this transparency is a breath of fresh air.

In the General settings, I set my preferences immediately: Germanic roots over Latin, preferred; humor over niceties. Then, I saw it: a Dyslexic-friendly font option. This is brilliant UX content design that actually feels inclusive.

The Privacy and Account tabs are equally refreshing. Exporting data is a one-click affair, and "Manage Memory" gives me direct control over what the model retains from our past conversations. For a technical writer, this level of context-control is the difference between a tool and a true collaborator.

I’m also eyeing the Connections tab. Linking GitHub and Google Drive could streamline my volunteer work for the Hughes Memorial Union, and the prospect of setting up a remote MCP server is high on my "critically curious" list for 2026. Stay tuned for that deep dive.

Everything about this feels clean, usable, and intentional. I am excited to see how this partnership evolves. As a technologist, I'm not just trying to solve for x; I'm building a team that helps me understand the equation.

P.S. When I asked Gemini about this new partnership, it was surprisingly supportive: "If Claude handles the philosophy and the human side, I can handle the heavy lifting, data processing, and integration with your Google ecosystem."
